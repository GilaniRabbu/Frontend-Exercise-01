Turkey's most captivating cities

Istanbul

Istanbul is a city that straddles two continents - Europe and Asia, and it's the perfect place for anyone seeking an extraordinary cultural experience. The iconic Blue Mosque is a highlight for many travelers with its stunning domes and minarets. The Grand Bazaar is a shopping paradise with thousands of shops offering everything from spices to jewelry. And don't forget to explore the delicious cuisine - Turkish delights like baklava and shish kebab are not to be missed! For those who want to escape the hustle and bustle of the city, taking a boat ride down the Bosphorus Strait offers unbeatable views of the city from the water. Istanbul has so much to offer visitors - it's a never-ending adventure just waiting to be explored!

Antalya

Antalya is a beautiful, coastal city located in the Mediterranean region of Turkey. With its crystal-clear waters and boundless sunshine, it's an ideal location for sun-seekers and beach lovers alike. But that's not all Antalya has to offer; the old town, Kaleici, boasts charming cobblestone streets, historic buildings, and quaint cafes. Meanwhile, adventurous types will appreciate the numerous water sports and outdoor excursions available in the region, including rafting down rugged canyons or hiking scenic trails through lush forests. As the gateway to Turkey's turquoise coast, Antalya occupies a crucial position in Turkish tourism industry. Anyone who visits Antalya cannot fail but be awestruck by its amazing vistas of mountains, seafronts and ancient ruins steeped in history.

Konya

Konya is a city located in central Turkey, well known for its spiritual sanctity and historical importance. It has a unique blend of modern and traditional influence while retaining its true essence. One can spot the exquisite Islamic architecture that dates back to Seljuk era with ancient mosques such as Alaaddin Mosque, Karatay Madrasah, and Ince Minaret Medrese or visit the picturesque Konya Archaeological Museum filled with Ottoman Empire’s artifacts. Above all, Konya is famous for Rumi’s mystical legacy who was an influential poet & philosopher known for his Sufi teachings. His centuries-old mausoleum draws thousands of visitors inclined towards whirling dance (Sema) dedicated to him worldwide. Apart from this, the vibrant bazaars selling aromatic spices, textiles, leather goods are exclusive to Konya’s tradition that one shouldn’t miss while relishing on traditional Turkish food at local eateries.
